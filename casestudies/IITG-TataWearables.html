WEX - Wearable & Exerciser

Design Brief

To Design a modular holistic health wearable. The focus has to be to optimise the wearable design for comfort, non-invasiveness, ease of use, communication and efficiency.
The wearable should sense/measure the following:

  • Sunlight exposure
  • Abdominal sounds, signals, and movements
  • Heart rate variability
  • Posture/stretching
  • Breathing
  • Blood Pressure

The sensors DO NOT have to be developed by the participant but adequate research needs to be done on available sensors that can be part of your wearable.

How Wex Works?

Two arrays of sensors are placed along the same artery to pick up signals. The distance between the two sensors and the time blood takes to travel between them is measured to obtain the pulse wave velocity. Pulse wave velocity is directly related to blood pressure.

The breathing rate is measured by detecting the change in pressure due to the movement of the diaphragm. Forposture the devices needs to be calibrated for neutral or idle posture initially. The change in posture is detected when the pressure measured if below the calibrated one and warning is given.

The pulse is detected with the help of LED and photodiode sensors. The LED light is shined into the skin. The scattered light is detected by the photodiode and the pulse rate is calculated. The heart rate is in direct relation with the pulse rate.

Vibration sensor calculates the abdominal sounds caused by the movement in the intestinal contents.
